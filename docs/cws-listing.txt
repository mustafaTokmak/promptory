Never lose a great prompt again.

Promptory captures every prompt you send to popular AI chatbots — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Grok, and Microsoft Copilot — and stores them privately on your device. Open the side panel, search your entire history, and paste the perfect prompt back into the active tool with one click.

🔒 100% LOCAL & PRIVATE
Your prompts never leave your browser. Everything is stored in local IndexedDB — no accounts, no cloud sync, no servers receiving your data. We literally can't read your prompts.

⚡ WORKS AUTOMATICALLY
No buttons to click, no copy-pasting. Promptory runs silently in the background and captures prompts as you type.

🔍 INSTANT SEARCH
Full-text search across all your saved prompts and responses. Find that perfect prompt from three weeks ago in under a second.

🏷️ TAGS & FILTERS
Auto-tagged by platform. Add your own tags to organize by project, client, or topic. Filter with one click.

📋 ONE-CLICK PASTE
Open the side panel, find your prompt, hit Paste — and it's injected directly into the active tool's input field. No clipboard needed.

📤 EXPORT YOUR LIBRARY
Download your entire prompt library as JSON or CSV at any time. Your data is always portable.

📊 FULL DASHBOARD
Open the library view for a full-width browsing experience with search, platform filters, favorites, and bulk actions.

── PERMISSIONS ──
• storage — saves prompts locally
• sidePanel — displays the side panel UI
• scripting — detects prompts and pastes them back
• Host permissions limited to the supported AI platforms only

Promptory is free with no account required.

 WHAT WAS FLAGGED (v1 → DON'T DO)
══════════════════════════════════════════════

Original v1 description had:
  - Body: "Every time you send a message to ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini,
    Perplexity, Grok, or Copilot, Promptory quietly saves it..."
  - Footer: "── SUPPORTED PLATFORMS ──
            ✓ ChatGPT (chatgpt.com)
            ✓ Claude (claude.ai)  ...etc"

Both blocks listed the same six brand names. CWS interpreted the
duplication as keyword stuffing and cited it under the
"misleading / excessive metadata" policy.

In v2, each brand is named exactly once (in the opening paragraph)
and the SUPPORTED PLATFORMS bullet list is removed entirely.
